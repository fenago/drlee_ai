**FROM: "Our models work in Jupyter but break in production"**

Your data scientists train models that achieve 95% accuracy in notebooks.

But when it's time to deploy:
- DevOps says "this isn't containerized properly"
- Models take 30 seconds to return predictions (users need 100ms)
- Silent model drift degrades accuracy to 60% over 3 months
- Re-training requires manual intervention every time
- Monitoring? Just logs. No drift detection. No explainability.
- Rollbacks break the entire pipeline

**Symptom**: Models rotting in notebooks. $400K/year ML engineers whose work never ships.

**TO: "We deploy ML models to production daily with 99.9% uptime"**

Your platform automatically:
- Packages models in optimized Docker containers
- Orchestrates training pipelines on Kubernetes with Kubeflow
- Tracks every experiment and model version with MLflow
- Serves models at <50ms latency with BentoML
- Detects data drift in real-time with Evidently
- Re-trains automatically when performance degrades
- Rolls back instantly if deployments fail
- Monitors everything with Prometheus and Grafana

**Outcome**: Production ML infrastructure shipping daily. One engineer doing what used to require entire teams.
